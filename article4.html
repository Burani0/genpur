Overcoming Obstacles: Thomas's Bright Future in the Classroom and Beyond

December 4, 2025 Success Stories GenPur CBO Team
Back to Articles

Meet Thomas, a remarkable 17-year-old who is redefining what's possible, proving that every child deserves the chance to shine.

Background and Initial Challenges

Thomas, who is in the sixth grade, was diagnosed with physical challenges at the age of 14 leading to amputation of his limbs. He lives with his parents and 7 other siblings - 4 sisters and 3 brothers. From the beginning, Thomas approached the world with curiosity, but the educational system wasn't always equipped to support his unique needs.

His primary challenges were not just physical, but also social and systemic. He struggled with mobility within a standard school environment, and the lack of accessible learning materials made classroom participation difficult. Furthermore, he often faced social isolation, finding it difficult to join in physical activities during break time or games time, which affected his confidence and engagement in school.

The core challenge was ensuring Thomas could access the same quality of education and social experiences as his peers without feeling limited by his condition. Due to lack of wheelchair to support him with mobility to school, Thomas missed school for two terms. His mum who is old and ailing was unable to escort him to school on a daily basis.

Support Provided

Through referral from ADRA organization where he is a beneficiary, GenPur partnered with Thomas's family and school to implement a comprehensive support system designed around his specific needs and strengths:

Mobility Assistance

We helped secure necessary mobility aid - a wheelchair for Thomas, enabling him to navigate his school environment independently and participate fully in classroom activities.

Mindset Change

Through ADRA, Thomas was taken through mindset change training and supported with a small grant to support him with his education. The grant included goat, fruit and tree seedlings whose aim is to serve as Income Generating Activities (IGAs) for him.

Outcomes and Future Outlook

The impact has been profound. Thomas is now back to school and thriving academically and socially. He is an enthusiastic member of the school Permaculture club where they are taught on entrepreneurship, social responsibility, and academic development and saving and spending.

Thomas has also started his own garden and currently farming jute leaves commonly known in the local language as "boo". Through this, he will sell the vegetables and save towards his school fees.

"Seeing Thomas come home excited about school every day is the greatest gift. The support didn't just change his school life; it changed our family's outlook on his future."
- Thomas's Mother

Thomas is now focused on his goal of becoming a doctor. His success story is a powerful reminder that with the right support, every child can achieve remarkable things.
